1. The FDA Price Decay Curve

PN = P0 × N-0.75

This interactive chart models price (P) based on the number of generic competitors (N). Real data shows that while a single generic barely moves the needle, having 6+ generic manufacturers drives the drug price down by roughly 80-90% from the monopoly baseline (P0).

↳ Source: U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). "Generic Competition and Drug Prices" (2019).

2. Welfare Distribution Models
  • Private Profit (Producer Surplus):
    (Price - Marginal Cost) × Quantity
  • Social Access (Consumer Surplus):
    ½ × (Max Willing Price - Price) × Quantity

Using standard microeconomic linear demand curves, the model visualizes how revoking a patent shifts economic value from corporate monopoly profit directly into public health access (consumer surplus).

↳ Source: Varian, H. R., "Microeconomic Analysis", W.W. Norton.
