1. The FDA Price Decay Curve

PN = P0 × N-0.75

This models price (P) based on generic competitors (N). Real data shows that while a single generic barely moves the needle, having 6+ generic manufacturers drives the drug price down by roughly 80-90%.

↳ Source: U.S. FDA. "Generic Competition and Drug Prices" (2019).

Microeconomic Modeling
The Linear Equations

This interactive graph uses standard inverse linear functions to plot the S&D curves:

  • Supply (S): P = (c - ΔS) + dQ
  • Demand (D): P = (a + ΔD) - bQ
  • Equilibrium: Q* = (a - c + ΔS + ΔD) / (b + d)
Empirical Proof

When India rejected pharmaceutical patents in the early 2000s, it caused a massive rightward shift in supply. Empirical studies show this competition eliminated monopoly deadweight loss and vastly expanded consumer welfare.

↳ Source: Chaudhuri, S., Goldberg, P. K., & Jia, P. (2006). "Estimating the Effects of Global Patent Protection in Pharmaceuticals..." The American Economic Review.

ICER Methodology & Survival Data
The ICER Formula

The Incremental Cost-Effectiveness Ratio (ICER) evaluates the economic value of an intervention:

ICER = (Δ Cost) / (Δ Effect)
Effect is measured in QALYs

By dropping the cost of the intervention while maintaining the exact same clinical effect (the molecule is identical), the generic alternative maximizes public health utility per dollar spent.

Kaplan-Meier Survival Data

Prior to Imatinib, the 5-year survival rate for CML was ~30%. With Imatinib, survival skyrocketed to nearly 90%, yielding roughly 5 additional QALYs per patient over baseline.

↳ Source: Hochhaus, A., et al. (2017). "Long-Term Outcomes of Imatinib Treatment for Chronic Myeloid Leukemia." The New England Journal of Medicine, 376(10), 917-927.
